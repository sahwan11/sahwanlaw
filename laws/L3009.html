Legislative Decree No. (30) of 2009 Establishing the Bahrain Chamber for Dispute Resolution (BCDR)

Current as of: 2026-04-20

Sahwan Law practitioner summary

The Bahrain Chamber for Dispute Resolution (BCDR), established by Decree-Law No. 30 of 2009 as amended by Decree-Law No. 64 of 2014 and Decree-Law No. 26 of 2021, is Bahrain's specialised forum for economic, financial, and investment disputes. BCDR operates in two tracks: the BCDR Arbitration Section administers arbitrations under its own institutional rules (often with AAA-ICDR administrative support for international cases), and the BCDR Court (previously known as "Section 9" or "Court of the BCDR") exercises MANDATORY jurisdiction over domestic commercial disputes exceeding a statutory monetary threshold to which the State or a government-owned entity is a party, or where both parties are commercial parties and the dispute value exceeds BHD 500,000 (after the 2021 amendment expanded this jurisdiction). Below the threshold, disputes remain with the ordinary High Civil Court. BCDR judgments and awards are subject to streamlined enforcement through the Civil and Commercial Execution Law (Decree-Law No. 22 of 2021). Parties cannot opt out of mandatory BCDR Court jurisdiction where the statutory criteria are met, making this a critical first check when drafting dispute-resolution clauses for Bahrain-governed or Bahrain-linked commercial contracts. Mediation and hybrid procedures are also available through BCDR's Mediation Centre.
