Legislative Decree No. (7) of 1987 Promulgating the Law of Commerce

Current as of: 2026-04-20

Sahwan Law practitioner summary

The Code of Commerce (Legislative Decree No. 7 of 1987) is the core merchant-law statute of Bahrain, sitting alongside (and largely independent of) the general Civil Code (DL 19/2001). It applies to traders (whether natural or legal persons) and to all commercial acts, even those performed by a non-trader. The Code is divided into books and chapters covering: general principles of commerce and the concept of a commercial act; the trader's status and obligations, the commercial register, and commercial books; the business enterprise (fonds de commerce) and its elements; commercial contracts including sale, carriage, commercial agency, commission, brokerage, deposit, pledge and insurance; banking operations (current accounts, deposits, loans, bank transfers, documentary credits, safe-deposit boxes); and commercial paper, in particular the bill of exchange, the promissory note and the cheque, with detailed rules on form, endorsement, acceptance, payment, recourse and protest. It also contains provisions on maritime and commercial insurance. The Code governs the substantive commercial relationship; procedural and enforcement aspects are routed through the Civil and Commercial Procedures Law, the Evidence Law, and (for insolvent traders) the Reorganisation and Bankruptcy Law (Law 22/2018). No pre-cutoff amendments are on record in this library. Current as of 2026-04-20, reflecting the pre-1 September 2025 cutoff state.
