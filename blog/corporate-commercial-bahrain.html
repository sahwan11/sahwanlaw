1. Types of Companies in Bahrain

Bahrain's Commercial Companies Law (Decree-Law No. 21 of 2001) provides several legal forms for businesses:

Company Type Minimum Capital Partners/Shareholders Liability
Limited Liability Company (WLL) BHD 50 2-50 partners Limited to shares
Closed Joint Stock (BSC Closed) BHD 250,000 2-50 shareholders Limited to shares
Public Joint Stock (BSC Public) BHD 1,000,000 7+ shareholders Limited to shares
General Partnership None 2+ partners Joint & several unlimited
Foreign Branch BHD 50,000 - Parent company

Note: The Limited Liability Company (WLL) is the most common structure. See our Company Registration Guide for details.

6. Frequently Asked Questions

What types of companies can be formed in Bahrain?

These include: Limited Liability Company (WLL), Closed and Public Joint Stock Companies, General Partnership, Limited Partnership, and Foreign Branch.

How does the M&A process work?

It involves: due diligence, LOI negotiation, Sale & Purchase Agreement, regulatory approvals, and transaction closing.

When does a company need restructuring?

For expansion, merger, financial difficulties, business model change, or preparation for sale or IPO.
